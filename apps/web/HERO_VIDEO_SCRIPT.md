# AplicoCV — Hero Advertisement Video
## Full Production Script & Shooting Storyboard

> **One-line concept:** A young Chilean remote worker, exhausted by manually retyping the same details into every job application, discovers AplicoCV — one click automates everything — and her frustration transforms into calm, efficient confidence.

## 3. LOCATION & SET DRESSING

**INT. HOME WORKSPACE — LATE MORNING**

- Bright, modern, minimal home desk by a large window with sheer curtains.
- **Silver Apple MacBook** open on a clean wooden desk. **⚠ The screen is NEVER visible to camera** — frame from behind/beside the lid, or angle the screen away. Focus stays on Sofía's face and hands.
- Set dressing: a leafy green plant, a warm ceramic coffee mug (steam visible early), a small notebook with handwritten sticky notes, a soft throw blanket, a cozy chair.
- Background: softly blurred (shallow depth of field) — warm home, plants, a hint of bookshelf.

---

## 4. VISUAL TREATMENT

- **Color arc:** Scenes 1–2 slightly cooler / flatter (the "grind"). From Scene 3 onward, warmth and saturation rise — sunlight grows, teal-blue UI accents glow. Scene 6–7 are the warmest, most golden.
- **Lighting:** Soft natural window key light from camera-left; gentle cool rim light for separation; subtle bounce fill. Cinematic, premium, never harsh.
- **Depth of field:** Shallow throughout — creamy bokeh, talent always tack-sharp.
- **Motion:** Smooth and deliberate — slow push-ins, one gentle orbit at the hero beat, soft pull-backs to reveal graphics. No jitter.

## 6. THE SHOOTING SCRIPT

> Voice-over is one **continuous track** across all scenes. Timecodes are targets; total VO ≈ 150 words.

---

### SCENE 1 — "THE GRIND"
**`[ 0:00 – 0:12 ]` · INT. HOME DESK — LATE MORNING**

**SHOT:** Slow push-in, medium close-up on Sofía hunched over the MacBook.

**ACTION:** She types hard and repetitively, brow furrowed. A tired sigh. She rubs her temple, glances at handwritten sticky notes, and retypes the same details yet again. Steam rises from a forgotten coffee.

**GRAPHICS (muted red, low energy):**
```
Applications: 3          ⏱ 4h 12m  (ticking slowly)
```

**SOFÍA (V.O. — weary):**
> "Every job application asked for the same thing — my name, my experience, my skills... over and over again."

**AUDIO:** Sparse, lonely piano. Repetitive keystroke SFX.

---

### SCENE 2 — "THE WALL"
**`[ 0:12 – 0:24 ]` · SAME — CONTINUOUS**

**SHOT:** Camera slowly drifts; shallow focus.

**ACTION:** Sofía slumps back, exhales hard, checks the time on her wrist. A beat of overwhelm — then a flicker of *"enough of this."* She pushes the sticky notes aside and sits up straighter.

**GRAPHICS:** The red `3` counter flickers and dims out.

**SOFÍA (V.O.):**
> "I'd spend my whole afternoon... and barely send three. There had to be a better way to apply to remote jobs."

**AUDIO:** Music holds on a single suspended note — the turning point.

### SCENE 3 — "THE DISCOVERY"

**SHOT:** Gentle push-in. Warm light begins rising across her face.

**ACTION:** Curiosity replaces fatigue. She leans toward the MacBook and starts using AplicoCV. Her eyebrows lift; the first genuine half-smile appears. Clean, glowing **teal** dashboard panels softly fade into the air around her.

**GRAPHICS:**
```

```

**SOFÍA (V.O. — brightening):**

## 11. PRODUCTION & DELIVERY NOTES

- **Clip-length reality:** Most text-to-video models cap at ~8–20s. Generate each of the 7 scenes as its own clip, then stitch under the single continuous VO track.
- **Continuity:** Lock Sofía's look from Shot 1's first frame; use it as the reference image for Shots 2–7 (Veo 3 / Kling reference mode) so face and wardrobe stay identical.
- **Graphics in post:** Shoot/generate Sofía photorealistically; composite all counters, rings, graphs, and the end-card in After Effects or CapCut for crisp legible numbers and exact brand colors.
- **Screen safety:** If a generator keeps revealing the laptop screen, add `back of laptop lid faces camera, screen blocked from view` to the prompt.
- **Hero-loop version:** Also export a silent, seamless 6–8s loop (Scenes 4–5) for muted autoplay behind the hero headline, with the full 90s available on click/scroll.

## 12. PRODUCT-ACCURACY NOTES (verified against the codebase)

Keep the ad truthful — these were checked against the actual product and must not be re-inflated:

- **Tagline is fixed brand copy:** *"Apply to every job that fits — in one click."* (rotating variants: *"in one click" / "tailored to you" / "automatically"*). Do **not** substitute invented taglines. `"Stop typing. Start applying."` is used only as the spoken campaign hook, not as the brand tagline on the end-card.
- **"One click" = autofill, not autonomous submit.** AplicoCV is a browser extension that **fills out** job-application forms (and, on Premium, prepares a tailored CV + cover letter for review). **The user always reviews and submits** — nothing is auto-submitted. The VO line *"it fills out every application for me"* is accurate; avoid stronger phrasing like *"it applies to jobs for you"* / *"applies automatically."*
- **Match score:** real and shown as a percentage; the on-screen `88%` mirrors the product's own showcase example. Do not use `98%` or other invented figures.
- **Dashboard stats are real metrics** (Total applications, Response rate, Interviews, Hours saved, Match/ATS score). The `47 / 88% / 12 hrs / 6` values are illustrative sample data — keep them plausible; consider a small *"Illustrative results"* disclaimer if required by ad platforms.
- **Audience:** the product serves **all** job seekers across 14+ portals (LinkedIn, Indeed, Workday, Get on Board, RemoteOK, We Work Remotely, etc.). Sofía-as-remote-worker is the **campaign persona** (per brief) and is supported — but don't claim the product is remote-only.
- **Languages:** EN / ES / PT-BR are all real. This script's VO is English per brief; ES and PT-BR voice/caption versions can reuse the same beats.
